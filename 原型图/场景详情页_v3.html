Disagree Appropriately

Disagreeing with a proposal without sounding dismissive

"Disagree without sounding discouraging."

Recommended Expressions

SOFT Ideal for superiors or initial discussions

"I see where you're coming from, and I'm wondering if we could also explore a few other perspectives on the timeline."

NEUTRAL Suitable for peer meetings or follow-ups

"I have some reservations about the current approach. Specifically, I'm concerned about the potential impact on resources."

FIRM Best for course correction or risk control

"To be honest, I'm not comfortable with this direction given the data we've seen. We should reconsider this before proceeding."

Usage & Risks

Risk Level: Moderate

When NOT to use:

Avoid excessive disagreement when the other party is being emotional or during the very early stages of informal brainstorming, as it may disrupt the creative flow.

Best Practices:

  • •Recommended for use during formal proposal reviews.
  • •Always follow a disagreement with a constructive suggestion.
